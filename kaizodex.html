🎯 Core Nuzlocke Rules

  • Permadeath — fainted = dead, box or release it.
  • First encounter only — one catch per route; miss it and you get nothing there.
  • Nickname everything — bonds you'll need.

🔒 Hardcore Additions

  • Level caps — never exceed the next boss's top level (enforced anyway).
  • SET battle mode — no switch previews.
  • No healing items in battle — held items (Sitrus/Lum) ARE allowed & essential.

⚔️ Common Clauses

  • Dupes clause — re-roll a first encounter you already own.
  • Shiny clause — shinies can always be caught.
  • No EV grinding — this hack gives no EVs. Buy cheap Rare Candies before the E4 to hit caps.

How the Enemy AI Thinks

The #1 reason blind runs die. Internalize these to predict enemy moves and bait mistakes.

Golden rule: load the enemy team in the damage calc and script the fight turn-by-turn before you play it. Improvising = dead mons.

🧠 How the AI actually chooses a move

Emerald Kaizo uses the "smart" AI on every trainer. Each turn it scores its moves and plays the best one, roughly in this priority:

  • 1. Can it KO? If a move kills you this turn, it almost always takes it — even a resisted move if that's the kill. Never sit in front of a boss mon that has a clean KO; you must out-speed, wall, or switch.
  • 2. Super-effective damage. Otherwise it picks its highest-damage move vs your current mon. It reads your typing, so it will click coverage (Charizard's EQ, Kingdra's HP Ground) into your switch-in.
  • 3. Setup / status when safe. Leads throw out Spikes, weather, screens, Thunder Wave, Hypnosis, Dragon Dance turn one if they don't fear you.
  • 4. It predicts switches. If it expects you to swap to a counter, it will often click the move that hits the counter, or double-switch to its own answer. Bringing a check in "for free" is rarely free.

🔄 Planning your switches (the core skill)

  • Switch on a forced/locked move. Safest switches are into a Choiced mon, a recharge turn (Hyper Beam/Sky Attack), or when the enemy is asleep/para-locked. Otherwise assume the AI hits your incoming mon.
  • Bait, don't hope. Send a mon that invites a predictable move (e.g. a Ground type invites a Grass/Water click), then swap to the mon that eats it. You're steering the AI, not gambling.
  • Sack intelligently. A doomed mon should die doing a job: chip the sweeper into KO range, set a screen, or paralyze — then bring your answer in on the free turn.
  • Respect priority. Aqua Jet, Mach Punch, Extremespeed, Ice Shard, Sucker Punch and Quick Attack ignore your Speed. A "safe" switch into a weakened mon can still die to priority.
  • Set mode is on. You don't get a free look when they send a mon out — know the roster from the Trainers tab first.

⚡ Abilities that punish switch-ins (Kaizo-changed)

Emerald Kaizo re-assigns abilities — several turn a naive switch into a disaster. See the full list in the Changes tab.

  • Intimidate — now on Manectric, Tyranitar, Arcanine, Houndoom, Glalie, Pinsir, Mightyena. Switching a physical attacker in gets its Attack dropped on entry; lead with special mons or setup that ignores it.
  • Lightningrod — Pikachu/Raichu, Manectric suck in your Electric moves (and get +SpA in later gens' logic — here it redirects/absorbs). Don't spam Electric into a Lightningrod holder.
  • Static / Flame Body — contact into Electrodes, Ponyta/Rapidash, Magcargo can cripple your sweeper with para/burn.
  • Rough Skin / Sturdy — Pineco/Forretress and Sturdy Rocks chip or survive your OHKO; don't rely on a single big hit.
  • Ditto has Shadow Tag — it traps you and copies your mon. Bring it in only on something it can't abuse, or a Ghost (immune to trapping is not automatic here — plan to KO it fast).
  • Swift Swim in rain — Kingdra, Ludicolo, Vaporeon, Swampert, Milotic, Whiscash double Speed in Wallace/Juan's rain and outrun your team. Break rain or hold priority/Scarf.
  • Speed Boost Torchic line note — only wild Torchic has Speed Boost (not the evolutions), minor for enemies but good to know.

☠️ Status & trapping to watch

  • Hypnosis / Sing / Grasswhistle appear on many bosses (Roxanne's Lunatone, Brawly's Poliwrath, Phoebe's Crobat, Juan's Vaporeon). Carry Lum Berries and sleep-immune / Early Bird mons.
  • Pursuit (Sidney, Phoebe, Houndoom) doubles up and traps fleeing mons — don't switch a chipped mon out into it; let it stay or sack it.
  • Destiny Bond (Phoebe's Gengar) kills whatever KOs it — chip it, then finish with a mon you can spare.
  • Explosion / Selfdestruct / Counter (Glacia's Regice/Wailord/Glalie, many Grounds) — don't wall a low-HP mon with your ace; they blow up for the trade.

Team Building & Encounter Routing

Route your catches around the early wall, then build coverage.

🥚 Starter Choice

Mudkip → Swampert is the safest anchor — Water/Ground bulk carries Roxanne, Wattson, Flannery and the mid-game. Treecko (Sceptile) is fast but frail; Torchic (Blaziken) nukes but is speed-checked and weak to common Rock/Water/Flying. Your rival always picks the type that beats yours. Check this version's starter abilities in EKHeX first.

🧭 Routing Priorities

  • Cover the early wall first (Roxanne → Brawly → Wattson → Flannery) before anything sentimental.
  • Build coverage, not a mono-team — reliable answers to Water, Ground, Flying, Psychic, Ice, Dragon, Steel by mid-game.
  • Value bulk + utility — recovery, status (T-Wave/Toxic/Spore), setup (DD/Calm Mind) and priority age well.
  • Keep a sacrifice slot — a throwaway KO buys a safe switch into a counter. Set party order before every boss.
